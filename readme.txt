maxxed out is a personality quiz that questions the culture of self-optimization and online identity. it asks you eight questions about your habits, interests, and lifestyle, then assigns you one of four archetypes based on how deep you are in a particular corner of internet culture.
the four types are:

looksmaxxer — obsessed with physical appearance and online comparison
systemaxxer — running life like a corporate metric, optimized to the point of emptiness
jestermaxxer — chronically a good time, zero direction
prophetmaxxer — too many opinions, not enough self-awareness

design process
the visual design is intentionally stripped back — plain white background, Times New Roman, blue hyperlinks. the goal was to make it feel like an early internet webpage, which felt right for a quiz that is poking fun at people who take the internet too seriously. no animations, no gradients, no dark mode. just text and boxes.
the quiz logic works by assigning each answer a type, tallying the scores across eight questions, and redirecting to a results page that reads from localStorage. each result has a short diagnosis and a set of tongue-in-cheek prescriptions.

My goal for the look was to recall web 1.0, I wanted to really question internet culture and I felt like scaling back the css to be very directly web oriented owuld be effective in this manner. 
If I had more time I would like to do some kind of percentage metric--like what level of maxx are you? I also think some more questions would be fun, however I really struggled coming up with them at the end.
